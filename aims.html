AIMS OF 'CRLR&SWA’

  1. One of the strategic aims of ‘CRLR&SWA’ is to develop a partnership between the govt. and private bodies like NGOs & CBOs, to provide a market-based solution that ensures a quality health-care services and products … accessible by the mass citizens at an affordable costs;
  2. Reaching the health-care products & services at the community door steps which is a crucial link between social stigma, community health and poverty. Logically speaking health-care facilities aren’t accessible by many of the adolescent girls and women because of the cultural-backwardness in a patriarchal society…especially if the health-care services are provided by male practitioners;
  3. Promoting a ‘Holistic Lifestyle’ by adopting a planet friendly sustainable practices of food productions…without using toxic substance that not only improve nutritional values but simultaneously improves soil health, harnessing renewable energy and promoting large-scale social forestry…keeping the carbon foot-print at a very low, harvesting and managing natural resources, water governance, waste management (W2W), using eco-friendly clothing while performing socio-cultural, religious functions etc;
  4. Promoting ‘Tobacco-free School Zones, Children’s Play Area with Recreational Activities’ encouraging kids of different faith to come, participate and form relationship …all these lead to develop a ‘Healthy Environment with Lasting Peace’ in the neighborhood, as these factors intrinsically impact the health (ESL) and future performances of the children!
  5. ‘Influencing the Decisions-Policy Makers and Community Institutes’ ensuring social and environmental justice based on equity, economy and sustainability, enabling all the stake-holders to participate in the decision making process, thus creating an opportunity for all to reduce carbon footprint and moving towards fulfilling SDGs by 2030.
