VISION

The ‘CRLR&SWA’ envisions a ‘just and informed society’, capable enough to develop a peaceful-healthy environment that sustains all sections of the community with mutual respect and co-operation, devoid of socio-cultural stigma and barriers of gender, religion or ethnicity.

MISSION

Process-driven duel missions of ‘CRLR&SWA’ are to end ‘Social Stigma & Economic Exclusion’ of the people considered ‘Untouchables’ through a process of institutional reformation; and to promote ‘People-Planet’s Holistic Health Management’ as preventive-care from further affecting the communities already gripped with illiteracy, ill-health and intergenerational poverty…with following measures:

  1. Ensuring ‘Social Justice for the People considered as Untouchables’: with compulsory education for children and access to equal employment opportunities. A specially designed education curriculum is developed to help the community members to reform socio-cultural & religious institutions…which have given birth to socio-religious stigma, fear, economic exclusion and social hierarchy…are the root causes of illiteracy, gender gap and intergenerational poverty resulting in high prevalence of Leprosy, HIV Positives, TB etc. It’s proven that an extra year of education for girl children substantially reduces early marriages, IMR and MMR, women’s ill-health, unexpected pregnancy, over population and income in-equality by about 43% and more. Women’s education further ensures baby’s brain development that determines their emotional and social learning skills - (ESL) etc that plays a crucial role in their adulthood;
  2. Developing ‘Collective Human Social Capitals & Intelligence’ – (CHSC&I): is a Social Engineering Process that funnels the community wisdom & strength while mobilizing, engaging and educating the citizens with the skills they need to find solutions locally by themselves to much of the challenges they face and develop resilience against the unsustainable use of natural resources that aggravates the challenges of poverty, climate change, environmental hazards and all water resources. The ‘CRLR&SWA’ takes these challenges head on while working with the leaders of the community institutes, decision makers, common citizens and the sufferers;
  3. ‘Re-building Local Economy’- (LED): as a priority using local resources. The ‘CRLR&SWA’ ensures ‘Financial Products & Services’ – (FPS) reaching the actual target beneficiaries. The underdeveloped local economy and financial non-inclusion are the major barriers against utilizing healthcare facilities, education and family nutritional needs of the marginalized. Thus the planned-support systems (LED & FPS) are meant to reduce mass-migration while seeking livelihood elsewhere and eventually end up as victims of human trafficking in person, flesh trading etc… invite HIV/AIDS and the like;
  4. Ensuring ‘Environmental Justice’ by promoting smart agricultural practices, large-scale ‘Social Forestry’ and teaches to harness ‘Renewable Energy’, ‘Sustainable Water Harvesting, recycling of ‘Farm Waste into Wealth’ (W2W) and ‘Food Production Techniques’ beginning with school children, youth associations, farming communities and public utility service providers. These practices not only improve livelihood but also preserve environment and soil health;
  5. Ensuring ‘Holistic Health Management’ (HHM): a preventive approach by raising ‘Ambassadors of Holistic Health’- (AHHM) who would ensure reaching the Concept & Practices of ‘HHM’ at the community door steps, which is the ultimate goal in bringing an innovation in health-care management system, targeting children and housewives to play the key-roles, since the health of human and other living beings is intrinsically connected with the health of the planet…as common source for sustenance of all life! Without social & political will it’s impossible to ensure ‘lasting peace’ which is a pre-condition to achieve ‘holistic health’. The ‘CRLR&SWA’ also educates & empowers women, adolescent girls and their youth partners to improve reproductive health, institutional delivery, hygiene, child-care and to end IMR, GB & domestic violence…;
  6. Influencing Decision Makers in the Govts: through civic education imparted through ‘Citizens’ Parliaments’, enabling grass-root people to participate in a free and fair decision making process to ensure many of their rights as part of good governance and equality before law. Social security and physical wellbeing of a person inter-linked with the environment that decides a person’s social & emotional health. Creating a tobacco-free school zone for the kids to breathe clean air is a right, but that needs political will and people’s right thinking to change the commercial interest of those engaged into it. The whole process makes the ‘Development Sustainable!’
